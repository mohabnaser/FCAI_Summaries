02

Decision Types and Techniques

Programmed vs Nonprogrammed decisions.

▣ Bases for Categorizing Decisions
  • How much time a manager must spend making them.
  • What proportion of the organization must be involved in making them.
  • Which organizational functions they focus on.
▣ Programmed vs Nonprogrammed
Type Meaning Traditional Techniques Modern Techniques
Programmed Routine and repetitive decisions. Organization develops specific processes for handling them. Habit · Clerical routine / standard operating procedures · Organization structure: common expectations, subgoals, information channels. Operations research: mathematical analysis models, computer simulation · Electronic data processing.
Nonprogrammed One-shot, ill-structured, novel policy decisions. Handled by general problem-solving processes. Judgment, intuition, creativity · Rules of thumb · Selection and training of executives. Heuristic problem-solving techniques applied to training human decision makers and constructing heuristic computer programs.
Repeated: Programmed = routine and repetitive. Nonprogrammed = one-shot decisions. Traditional programmed technique = habit.

04

Consensus

تعريف وميزة وعيب. العيب متكرر جدا.

Consensus
An agreement on a decision by all the individuals involved in making that decision.
Consensus يعني كل المشاركين موافقين على القرار.

Advantages

  • It focuses "several heads" on the decision.
  • Employees are more likely to be committed to implementing a decision if they helped make it.

Disadvantage

It often involves time-consuming discussions relating to the decision.

06

The Rational Decision-Making Process

النموذج الرسمي وخطواته وافتراضاته.

▣ Model
Identify existing problem
->
List alternative problem solutions
->
Select most beneficial alternative
->
Implement chosen alternative
->
Gather problem-related feedback
Exam trap: Implementation is not the final step; gathering problem-related feedback comes after implementation.
▣ Assumptions
  1. Humans are economic beings with the objective of maximizing satisfaction or return.
  2. All alternatives and their possible consequences are known.
  3. Decision makers have some priority system to guide them in ranking the desirability of each alternative.
▣ Limitations on Alternatives
  1. Authority factors.
  2. Biological or human factors.
  3. Physical factors.
  4. Technological factors.
  5. Economic factors.
Discretionary area: acceptable courses of action limited by legal restrictions, moral and ethical norms, formal policies and rules, and unofficial social norms.
Number of alternatives: More alternatives usually lead to more effective decisions, but too many alternatives may demotivate decision makers.
▣ Evaluating and Implementing Alternatives
  1. List the potential effects of each alternative.
  2. Assign a probability factor to each potential effect.
  3. Compare each alternative's expected effects and the respective probabilities of those effects.

After selection, develop an action plan to implement the chosen alternative, giving it all supportive chances of success. Feedback determines the implemented alternative's effect on the problem. If the problem is not solved, seek out and implement another alternative.

07

Decision-Making Conditions: Risk and Uncertainty

فرق مهم ومباشر في Mid 2022.

Risk
Situations in which statistical probabilities can be attributed to alternative potential outcomes.
مثال: Alpha Inc. فتحت 10 branches في الصين قبل كده، فعندها خبرة واحتمالات.
Uncertainty
Situations where the probability that a particular outcome will occur is not known in advance.
مثال: Beta Inc. ليس لديها خبرة سابقة في الصين.
08

Decision-Making Tools

Subjective vs Objective tools, probability theory, and decision trees.

▣ Tool Types
Subjective Tools Objective Tools
Intuition · Experience · Rule of thumbs Probability Theory · Decision Trees
▣ Probability Theory

A decision-making tool used in risk situations. Probability refers to the likelihood that an event or outcome will actually occur.

EV = I * P
  • EV: expected value for the alternative considered.
  • I: income that the alternative would produce.
  • P: probability of producing that income.
Alternative Potential income Probability Expected value
A $90,000 .2 $18,000
B $75,000 .4 $30,000
C $60,000 .8 $48,000
▣ Decision Trees
Decision Tree
A graphic decision-making tool typically used to evaluate decisions involving a series of steps.
Example: Stygian Chemical Company deciding whether to build a small or large plant for a new product with an expected life of 10 years.
Build Big Plant
->
High Average Demand
Low Average Demand
High Initial / Low Subsequent
Build Small Plant
->
Initially High Demand
->
Expand or No Change
->
High / Low Demand
If demand is initially low, the small plant branch remains with initially low demand.
  1. Study investment amounts necessary for building a large plant, building a small plant, and expanding a small plant.
  2. Weigh the probabilities of facing different product demand levels for various decision alternatives.
  3. Consider projected income yields for each decision alternative.
Net Expected Gain = Expected Value - Investment Cost
09

Group Decision Making

Advantages, disadvantages, groupthink, brainstorming, nominal group technique, and Delphi.

▣ Group Decision Making: Pros and Cons

Advantages

  • A group can generally come up with more and better decision alternatives.
  • A group can draw on collective, diverse organizational experiences.
  • Members tend to support implementation more fervently.

Disadvantages

  • It takes longer because groups must discuss all members' views.
  • Group decisions cost more because they take the time of more people.
  • Groupthink: members' efforts to maintain friendly relationships among themselves.
▣ Brainstorming
Group members state ideas
->
No comments at this stage
->
Leader records ideas
->
Ideas evaluated after all recorded
Advantage Disadvantage
Encouraging the expression of as many useful ideas as possible. Wasting the group's time on ideas that are wildly impractical.
▣ Nominal Group Technique (NGT)
  1. Each group member writes down individual ideas on the decision or problem.
  2. Each member presents individual ideas orally; ideas are usually written on a board.
  3. After all members present ideas, the group discusses these ideas simultaneously; discussion tends to be unstructured and spontaneous.
  4. When discussion is completed, a secret ballot is taken. The idea receiving the most votes is adopted and implemented.
Advantage Disadvantage
Secret ballot allows individuals to support or reject an idea without fear of recrimination. Group members have no way of knowing why individuals voted the way they did.
Exam trap: In NGT, group members do meet face to face. "Never meet face to face" is Delphi, not NGT.
▣ Delphi Technique
  1. A problem is identified.
  2. Group members provide anonymous responses to a carefully designed questionnaire.
  3. Responses are compiled and sent to all group members.
  4. Members generate new individual solutions after studying the compiled responses.
  5. Steps 3 and 4 are repeated until a consensus problem solution is reached.
Advantage Disadvantage
Ideas can be gathered from members who are too geographically separated or busy to meet face to face. Members are unable to ask questions of one another.

Question: State one advantage and one disadvantage for the following group decision techniques: Brainstorming, Nominal Group Technique, Delphi Technique.
