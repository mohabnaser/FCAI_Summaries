## Page 6
Definitions 
• Controlling:
The process managers go through to control.
• According to Roberto Mockler, controlling is
a systematic effort by business management to compare performance to 
predetermined standards, plans, or objectives to determine whether 
performance is in line with these standards and presumably to take any 
remedial action required to see that human and other corporate resources 
are being used in the most effective and efficient way possible in achieving 
corporate objectives.

## Page 10
The Controlling Process
Measuring Performance. 
• How to Measure:
1. Establish units of measure that represent target performance.
2. Determine the level of each of these units accomplished by each 
target employee.
• What to Measure:
• Managers must always keep in mind that a wide range of 
organizational activities can be measured as part of the control 
process.

Power and Control
Total Power of a Manager. 
Total Power = Position Power + Personal Power
1. Position Power:
Is power derived from the organizational position a manager holds.
2. Personal Power:
Is power derived from a manager’s relationships with others.
