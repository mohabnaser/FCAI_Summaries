## Page 34
Contingency Approach
Fundamentals of Management History and Current Thinking 
What managers do in practice depends on, or is contingent upon, a 
given set of circumstances
“If … Then” Relationship 
If a manager has a group of inexperienced subordinates, then the 
contingency approach would recommend that he or she lead in a 
different fashion than if the subordinates were experienced

## Page 35
Contingency Approach
Fundamentals of Management History and Current Thinking 
It is an approach that attempts to outline the conditions or situations in 
which various management methods have the best chance of success
There is probably no one best way to solve all management problems 
in all organizations
There is probably one best way to solve any given management 
problem in any one organization

• Founder: Ludwig von Bertalanffy (Physics & Biology Scientist)
• To fully understand the operation of an entity, the entity must be 
viewed as a system
• System: is a number of interdependent parts functioning as a whole 
for some purpose
• Example: Human Body {Ears, Eyes, Brain}
• System theory integrates the knowledge of various specialized fields 
so that the system as a whole can be better understood.
