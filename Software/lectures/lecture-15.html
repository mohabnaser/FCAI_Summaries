01

What Are Ethics?

Definitions from philosophy → computer ethics → SE ethics.

📖 Definitions
  • Ethics = "moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity".
  • Or "the branch of knowledge that deals with moral principles".
  • Origin: Greek ethos = habitual character, custom.

Computer Ethics

"The field of analyzing and solving dilemmas and ethical issues arising during using and developing computing systems in the light of ethical theories and professional practice."

👔 What is a Professional?

A professional = member of a profession defined by:

  • Set of knowledge and skills acquired through education + training.
  • License to practice the profession.

Professionalism Means...

  • Competence · Honesty · Integrity
  • Reliability and responsibility
  • Communication and collaboration
  • Self-development
  • Good judgment
02

Theories of Ethics

4 main schools of ethical thought.

🎯 Consequences Theory

Egoism: action good/bad based on benefit/harm to individual.

Utilitarianism: morally acceptable if it brings the greatest good for the greatest number.

⚖️ Deontology (Duty-based)

Actions are what matters — NOT consequences. Humans have duty to do what is good. Actions are good/bad in themselves.

🌍 Relativism

No universal ethical norms. Right and wrong differ between cultures, societies, generations.

🕊️ Divine Command

Humans are on Earth to serve God. Divine guidance is the source of ethics — defines what is right and wrong.

03

Why Do We Need SE Ethics?

Unethical practices cause SW failures, project failures, and death.

⚠️ Real Examples
  • London Ambulance System — Company took work it had no experience in. System released with known bugs.
  • The Vasa (classical engineering ethics).
  • Therac-25 — Company denied the system had problems.
  • Royal Mail / Fujitsu (UK, 1999-2021) — Faulty accounting software accused 700+ post office workers of theft.
❓ Ethical Questions
  1. Should you work on a product used in spying on political opponents?
  2. Should you release a safety-critical product not fully tested because your manager pressures you?
  3. Should you accept contracts you're not competent to do?
  4. Should you sacrifice good SE practices to finish on time?
  5. What if the project is behind schedule and your employer lies to the client?
  6. What if the client approaches you for private work behind your employer's back?

SE Code of Ethics — Preamble

ACM/IEEE joint code. Authoritative reference for the profession.

📜 Who is a SE?

"Software Engineers are those who contribute by direct participation or by teaching, to the analysis, specification, design, development, certification, maintenance, and testing of software systems."

"Because of their roles, software engineers have significant opportunities to do good or cause harm."

🎯 Code Scope

The Code applies to:

  • Practitioners
  • Educators
  • Managers
  • Supervisors
  • Policy makers
  • Trainees and students of the profession

🌍 Principle 1 — Public

"Software engineers shall act consistently with the public interest."

  • Accept full responsibility for their work
  • Moderate interests of SE / employer / client / users with public good
  • Only approve software if good, safe, well-tested
  • Disclose any harm the software may cause
  • Be fair, avoid deception
  • Consider physical disabilities
💼 Principle 2 — Client & Employer

"Act in the best interests of client and employer, consistent with public interest."

  • 2.01 — Provide services only in your area of competence
  • 2.02 — Do not use illegally obtained software
  • 2.03 — Use property as authorized by owner
  • 2.04 — Ensure documents you rely on are approved
  • 2.05 — Honor confidentiality of information
  • Be honest about your limitations · Report violations · Accept no harmful work
📦 Principle 3 — Product

"Ensure products and modifications meet the highest professional standards."

  • 3.02 — Ensure proper and achievable goals
  • 3.04 — Ensure they are qualified for any project
  • 3.07 — Strive to fully understand specifications
  • 3.09 — Ensure realistic quantitative estimates (cost, scheduling)
  • 3.10 — Ensure adequate testing, debugging, and review ⭐
  • Plus: project mgmt, methodology use, documentation, privacy, data legitimacy
⚖️ Principle 4 — Judgment

"Maintain integrity and independence in their professional judgment."

  • 4.03 — Maintain professional objectivity in evaluations
  • 4.04 — Not engage in deceptive financial practices (bribery, double billing)
  • 4.05 — Disclose conflicts of interest that cannot reasonably be avoided
  • 4.06 — Refuse to participate in bodies with undisclosed conflicts
👔 Principle 5 — Management

"SE managers and leaders shall subscribe to and promote an ethical approach to management."

  • 5.01 — Ensure good management, effective procedures for quality and risk reduction
  • 5.02 — Inform SEs of standards before holding them accountable
  • 5.03 — Inform SEs of password/file/confidential info policies
  • 5.04 — Assign work only after considering education and experience
  • 5.05 — Ensure realistic estimates of cost, scheduling, quality
  • 5.06 — Attract SEs only by accurate description of employment conditions
  • 5.07 — Offer fair and just remuneration
  • 5.08 — Not unjustly prevent someone from a suitable position
  • 5.10 — Provide due process in hearing violations
  • 5.11 — Not ask a SE to do anything inconsistent with this Code ⭐
🎓 6 — Profession

"Advance the integrity and reputation of the profession consistent with public interest."

🤝 7 — Colleagues

"Be fair to and supportive of colleagues."

  • 7.02 — Assist colleagues in professional development
  • Encourage following code
  • Credit fully the work of others
  • Review objectively
📚 8 — Self

"Participate in lifelong learning."

  • 8.01 — Knowledge of dev, maintenance, testing
  • 8.02 — Improve ability to create safe, reliable, useful SW
  • 8.03 — Improve documentation
  • 8.04 — Understand SW environments
  • 8.05 — Knowledge of standards and laws
  • 8.06 — Knowledge of this Code itself

Case Studies

LAS · CONFIRM · Royal Mail/Fujitsu — لمن نطبق الـ principles.

🚑 London Ambulance System

The LAS automated system was deployed and failed catastrophically. The inquiry findings exposed multiple ethical violations:

  • Schedule was set politically, not technically — unrealistic.
  • Bid evaluation team had little experience or motivation.
  • System Options had no prior experience — yet tendered cheapest bid and won (violates 2.01).
  • Did not honestly report status to NHS management (violates 4.05).
  • System was not properly tested, had serious errors (violates 3.10).
  • "Go live" decision was political, not based on QA evaluation.
  • Staff were not sufficiently trained, no backup plan.

✈️ CONFIRM Project (ARMIS + Marriott + Hilton)

ARMIS (owned by AMR — same company that built SABRE) built a hotel/car rental reservation system. Started 1988, cancelled 1992. Never delivered anything working.

  • ARMIS always lied about the status of the system (violates Principle 4).
  • Initial $55M investment → $125M.
  • Court cases settled with ARMIS paying $160M.

📮 Royal Mail / Fujitsu (1999-2021)

British Royal Mail deployed Fujitsu accounting software in 1999. Between 2000-2014:

  • 700+ post office workers wrongly accused of theft.
  • Many imprisoned, ruined financially, committed suicide.
  • 2021: Court ruled software was faulty. £58M compensation to 555 employees.
  • Developers said code had no review, no coding style, many bugs.
  • Managers threatened developers ("continue or be fired") — violates 5.11.
  • Developers violated 3.10 (ensure adequate testing).
  • Managers violated 5.01, 5.05, 5.11.

Question 1 Fall 2021–22 · Q1.19
Case: Mina works for public schools IT. He's friends with Aziz, uncle of Rami who owns FCAI Soft. Testing showed FCAI Soft's product is below standard. As a favor for Aziz, Mina accepts the bad software. Rami will hire Mina's brother in return.

Which clauses of Principle 4 (Judgment) are violated?
4.03 — Maintain professional objectivity
4.04 — No bribery/improper financial practices
4.05 — Disclose conflicts of interest
4.06 — Refuse private/governmental bodies with conflicts
A Only 4.03, 4.06
B Only 4.03, 4.04, 4.05
C Only 4.04, 4.05, 4.06
D All of them
✅ الإجابة الصح: B — 4.03, 4.04, 4.05
  • 4.03 ❌ — Accepted bad software despite knowing — lacks objectivity.
  • 4.04 ❌ — Receiving job for brother = bribery / improper financial practice.
  • 4.05 ❌ — Did not disclose the conflict of interest (Aziz, Rami).
  • 4.06 — Not directly applicable (no formal body involved).
Question 2 Fall 2021–22 · Q1.20
Case: Samir is great in Java but no C#/.NET experience. Client wants C#/.NET project — business secret, signed NDA. Samir accepts. Tells friend everything about project (NDA violation). Friend gives him cracked Visual Studio. Which Principle 2 clauses did Samir violate?
2.01 — Provide services only where competent
2.02 — Do not use illegally obtained software
2.03 — Only use property as authorized
2.04 — Documents relied on are approved
2.05 — Honor confidentiality of information
A 2.01, 2.02, 2.03
B 2.01, 2.04, 2.05
C 2.01, 2.02, 2.05
D 2.02, 2.03, 2.04
E All of them
✅ الإجابة الصح: C — 2.01, 2.02, 2.05
  • 2.01 ❌ — Not competent in C#/.NET, accepted anyway.
  • 2.02 ❌ — Used cracked Visual Studio = illegally obtained software.
  • 2.05 ❌ — Shared NDA-protected project details with friend.
Question 3 Winter 2021 · Q1.19
In the Royal Mail / Fujitsu case (faulty accounting software, 700+ employees accused, suicides), which rules were violated by the Fujitsu developers?
A 3.04 — Ensure they are qualified
B 3.09 — Realistic cost/schedule estimates
C 3.02 — Proper and achievable goals
D 3.07 — Fully understand specifications
E 3.10 — Ensure adequate testing, debugging, and review
✅ الإجابة الصح: E — 3.10 (Adequate Testing) Developers said: code wasn't reviewed, no coding style, testing showed many bugs but management forced delivery anyway. Direct violation of 3.10.
Question 4 Winter 2021 · Q1.20
In the same case, which rule was violated by Fujitsu project managers?
A 5.02 — Ensure SEs are informed of standards
B 5.04 — Assign work based on experience
C 5.06 — Accurate description of employment
D 5.07 — Fair remuneration
E 5.11 — Not ask SE to do anything inconsistent with this Code
✅ الإجابة الصح: E — 5.11 Managers threatened developers ("continue or get fired") to release buggy code — explicitly inconsistent with the Code (3.10 on testing).
Question 5 Winter 2025 · Q1.14
Sami discovered a vulnerability in healthcare software that could expose patient data. Fixing it would delay delivery. Manager threatens to fire him if not released on time. Which Principle 5 rules did the manager violate?
A 5.03, 5.08, 5.10
B 5.01, 5.02, 5.03
C 5.03, 5.05, 5.10
D 5.01, 5.05, 5.11
✅ الإجابة الصح: D — 5.01, 5.05, 5.11
  • 5.01 ❌ — No good management for quality/risk reduction (releasing with vulnerability).
  • 5.05 ❌ — Unrealistic estimates (no time to fix the bug properly).
  • 5.11 ❌ — Asking SE to do something inconsistent with the Code (release insecure software).
Question 6 Winter 2025 · Q1.19
A SE realizes he needs to learn a new security framework to keep up with industry. He enrolls in an online course. Which Principle 8 (Self) rules does he apply?
8.01 — Further knowledge of analysis/dev/maintenance/testing
8.02 — Improve ability to create safe, reliable, useful SW at reasonable cost & time
8.03 — Improve documentation
8.04 — Understand SW environments
8.05 — Knowledge of relevant standards and law
8.06 — Knowledge of this Code itself
A 8.01 and 8.02
B 8.03 and 8.04
C 8.01 and 8.05
D Only 8.06
✅ الإجابة الصح: A — 8.01 and 8.02 Learning a new security framework directly applies:
  • 8.01 ✅ — Furthering knowledge (security in dev/maintenance).
  • 8.02 ✅ — Improving ability to create safe, reliable software.

The framework isn't a law/standard (so 8.05 doesn't apply directly).

Question 7 Winter 2025 · Q1.20
A SE realizes her company doesn't adopt any code of ethics. She studies the SE Code of Ethics on her own and applies it in her work and tells her colleagues. Which Principle 8 (Self) rule does she apply?
A 8.01 and 8.02
B 8.03 and 8.04
C 8.01 and 8.05
D Only 8.06
✅ الإجابة الصح: D — Only 8.06 Studying and applying this very Code of Ethics = clause 8.06 ("Improve their knowledge of this Code, its interpretation, and its application").
Question 8 Practice — comprehensive
Which principle requires that SEs provide services only in their area of competence?
A Principle 1 — Public
B Principle 2 — Client and Employer (specifically 2.01)
C Principle 4 — Judgment
D Principle 6 — Profession
✅ الإجابة الصح: B — Principle 2 (2.01) "Provide services only where competent" is part of duty to client and employer. Also 3.04 covers a similar idea for product quality.
